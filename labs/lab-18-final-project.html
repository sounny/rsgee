Lab 18 - Final Project

Design and execute a remote sensing analysis using Google Earth Engine that addresses a real-world question. This is your capstone for the course.

Overview

Your project can be a paper or an app that demonstrates a complete workflow: problem framing, data selection, method, results, and discussion. Aim for clarity and reproducibility.

Requirements

  • Topic: Any domain covered in class (e.g., land cover, rivers, wildfires, health, agriculture)
  • Data: Use at least one satellite image collection; cite sources
  • Methods: Apply 2+ core methods (indices, reduction, classification, zonal stats, time series, etc.)
  • Outputs: Maps/figures and a short table of key metrics
  • Reproducibility: Share a viewable Earth Engine script URL and describe steps

Deliverables

  1. Report (4–6 pages)
    • Title, author, and abstract (150–250 words)
    • Introduction and research question
    • Data and study area
    • Methods (include key code snippets/flow)
    • Results (maps, charts, tables)
    • Discussion (limitations, future work)
    • References
  2. Appendix: EE script URL(s) and instructions to reproduce
  3. Optional App: A simple GEE App with UI controls demonstrating your workflow

Rubric (100 points)

  • Problem framing and relevance: 15
  • Data selection and preprocessing: 15
  • Method correctness and justification: 25
  • Results quality (maps/figures/tables): 25
  • Reproducibility and clarity: 10
  • Writing quality and references: 10

Suggested Timeline

  1. Week 1: Topic selection and data scouting
  2. Week 2: Prototype analysis and early figures
  3. Week 3: Full analysis and results
  4. Week 4: Writing, polishing, and submission

Submission

Subject: Final Project - [Your Name]

  • Attach your PDF report
  • Include EE script URL(s) with viewer access
  • Include link to your GEE App (if created)
