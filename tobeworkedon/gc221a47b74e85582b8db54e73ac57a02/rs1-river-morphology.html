In this lab, we provide ways in which Earth Engine can be used to aid river planview morphological studies. In the first half of the lab, we show how to distinguish river pixels from other types of water bodies and how to extract river centerline, river width, bank aspect, and length. In the second half of the chapter, we give examples of applying these methods to multitemporal image collections to estimate changes in river widths for rivers with stable channels and to estimate bank erosion for rivers that tend to meander quickly. The analysis uses both raster- and vector-based methods provided by Earth Engine to help quantify river morphology. More importantly, these methods can be applied at scale.
