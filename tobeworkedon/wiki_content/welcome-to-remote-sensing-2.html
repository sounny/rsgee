Welcome to Class

Welcome to an exciting journey through Remote Sensing and Digital Image Processing! This course is designed for both undergraduate and graduate students, under the names "Digital Image Processing" for undergraduates and "Remote Sensing" for graduates. The terminology naming the course is a good reflection of what we will be doing; in essence, we are processing imagery from satellites to get a better understanding of a Big Blue World.

The course goes beyond traditional remote sensing, extending into web programming and integrating cutting-edge technologies like Google Earth Engine. For those of you who may not be familiar, Google Earth Engine is a transformative tool in the field of remote sensing, allowing us to work with massive datasets and powerful cloud-based computational resources.

Are you worried about the programming component? Don't be! This course is designed to be inclusive of all skill levels. Even if you've never written a line of code, you'll find the environment friendly and conducive to learning. We'll be looking at a lot of great topics from an introductory perspective, like big data, artificial intelligence, and web visualizations, all while equipping you with the programming skills essential for modern GIS and remote sensing careers.

We find ourselves in an era where the landscape of GIS and remote sensing is evolving rapidly. The course will give you a front-row seat to this transformation from desktop-based systems to cloud computing and back again. This is more than just a class; it's your gateway to understanding and participating in the ongoing revolution in remote sensing and geospatial technologies!  I look forward to digital exploring the world with you this semester.
