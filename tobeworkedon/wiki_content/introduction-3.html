Rivers are not just water channels; they are dynamic systems that change shape, size, and direction due to various natural processes. Understanding these changes is crucial for various fields like environmental science, urban planning, and even disaster management. In geography, these studies happen in fluvial geomorphology, a subdiscipline of geomorphology that studies how rivers shape the Earth's surface. It focuses on understanding how river processes change over time.

In this lab, we aim to leverage the power of Earth Engine to explore some key aspects of river systems. You'll learn how to distinguish a river from other water bodies using satellite imagery. We'll also get into the weeds of river morphology by calculating its centerline and width.  Then, we will go a step further to analyze how rivers change over time by calculating bank erosion rates.

By the end of this lab, you'll have a good grasp of how to:

  • Distinguish rivers from other water bodies
  • Perform basic morphological analyses on rivers
  • Detect changes in river forms over time

Core Concepts

  • Working with Landsat surface water products: Learn how to utilize Landsat data to study surface water changes.
  • Calculating river centerline and width: Utilize Earth Engine functions to compute the spatial characteristics of rivers.
  • Quantifying river bank erosion: Detect and measure changes in the river bank over time.

Reinforced Skills

Throughout this lab, you will also be reinforcing several essential Earth Engine skills:

  • Import images and image collections, filter, and visualize: Learn to bring in various types of imagery and refine them for your specific study area.

  • Perform basic image analysis: Get hands-on experience selecting bands, computing indices, and creating masks to help you focus on your area of interest.

  • Perform image morphological operations: Understand and apply various image processing techniques to highlight or suppress features in the data.

  • Write a function and map it over an ImageCollection: Discover the power of applying a function across a series of images to analyze changes over time.

  • Use reduceRegions to summarize an image with zonal statistics in irregular shapes: Learn how to perform zonal statistics that can summarize image data based on irregularly shaped regions.

  • Work with vector data: Grasp the basics of working with vector layers, which is essential for many types of geospatial analyses.
