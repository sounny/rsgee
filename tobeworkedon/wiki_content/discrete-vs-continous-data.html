Discrete data refers to data that can only take on certain numerical values, often integers. This type of data is limited to a finite number of possible outcomes. It is typically used to represent counts or whole numbers, such as the number of people in a household or the number of students in a class. On the other hand, continuous data can take on any value within a given range or interval. This type of data is often measured and can include fractions or decimals, such as temperature or weight.

In the context of machine learning, regression models predict continuous variables, whereas classification models predict discrete or categorical variables with a finite number of categories. For example, a regression model may be used to predict the price of a house based on its features, whereas a classification model may be used to predict whether a customer will purchase a product based on their demographic information.

In remote sensing, image classification is the process of categorizing every pixel in an image into a limited number of labeled land cover and/or land use classes. This resulting classified image provides a simplified thematic map.  In this way, classification is taking a continuous dataset, a remote sensing image, and turning it into a discrete dataset, a thematic map.
