These datasets are valuable in remote sensing for monitoring and analyzing fire events like the Bobcat Fire. These datasets' different resolutions and update frequencies offer a comprehensive view, ranging from detailed local analyses (500 m resolution) to broader regional monitoring (2 km, every 15 minutes). The temporal coverage allows for historical comparisons and trend analysis. The MODIS datasets provide both active fire locations and post-fire burned area assessments. In contrast, the GOES datasets offer more frequent updates for active fire monitoring, which is particularly beneficial for real-time applications and decision-making.
