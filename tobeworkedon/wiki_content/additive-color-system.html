In this module, we will look at The Additive Color System, a method used in digital displays to create colors by mixing varying levels of Red, Green, and Blue (RGB) light. Each color is represented by a three-digit number that forms a pixel. Adjusting these RGB values allows for a wide range of colors, from black (0, 0, 0) to white (255, 255, 255). In Earth Engine and other digital displays, the data is shown using these RGB channels. Each band's Digital Number (DN) influences the pixel's color displayed. If one color channel's DN is higher, the pixel will appear tinted in that color. The system also explains how primary colors combine: red and green make yellow, green and blue make cyan, and red and blue make magenta. Understanding this system is key for interpreting digital imagery, including satellite images.
